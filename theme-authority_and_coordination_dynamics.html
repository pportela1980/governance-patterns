Authority and Coordination Dynamics

The Bottleneck Leader
(Block)
The Bottleneck Leader

Description: Centralizing leadership to manage decision overload leads to dependency.

Problem: In rapidly evolving environments, decision-making can become overwhelming. Individuals feel lost without clear direction.

Solution: Centralize power in a single leader who “keeps things organized,” which, however, creates stagnation, dependency, and risks burnout or authoritarianism.

Resources: nan
Related Patterns:
Shared Stewardship, Consent, Not Consensus, Power as Resource, Not Authority, Right Decision, Right Level, Naming the Invisible
The Gatekeeper Effect
(Block)
The Gatekeeper Effect

Description: Filtering access in growing organizations concentrates control.

Problem: As organizations expand, filtering access to key resources becomes challenging, leading to a few individuals or groups becoming de facto gatekeepers.

Solution: The attempt to maintain quality ends up restricting participation and innovation by concentrating control.

Resources: nan
Related Patterns:
Shared Stewardship, Making Power Visible, Participation with Purpose
The Cult of the Founder
(Block)
The Cult of the Founder

Description: Charismatic leadership creates dependency and stifles succession.

Problem: When groups form around a charismatic leader, power becomes overly concentrated, stifling governance evolution and suppressing succession.

Solution: Dependency on a single figure limits fresh ideas and undermines distributed leadership.

Resources: nan
Related Patterns:
Shared Stewardship, Right Decision, Right Level, Naming the Invisible
Shared Stewardship
(Unblock)
Shared Stewardship

Description: Rotates leadership roles to distribute power and enhance inclusivity.

Problem: Centralized leadership leads to burnout and exclusion by confining decision-making to a small group.

Solution: Rotate leadership roles and share responsibility across the community to distribute power, enhance resilience, and encourage inclusivity.

Resources: nan
Related Patterns:
The Bottleneck Leader, The Cult of the Founder, Consent, Not Consensus, Power as Resource, Not Authority
Consent, Not Consensus
(Unblock)
Consent, Not Consensus

Description: Uses consent-based decision-making to balance inclusion with action.

Problem: Striving for full consensus can slow decisions and marginalize minority viewpoints, leading to ineffective, watered-down outcomes.

Solution: Adopt a consent-based approach—“good enough for now, safe enough to try”—to balance inclusivity with timely action.

Resources: nan
Related Patterns:
Shared Stewardship, Right Decision, Right Level, Naming the Invisible
Power as Resource, Not Authority
(Unblock)
Power as Resource, Not Authority

Description: Reframes power as enabling collective action rather than control.

Problem: Viewing power solely as control creates hierarchical bottlenecks and undermines collaborative efforts.

Solution: Reframe power as a resource that supports collective action, care, and innovation rather than serving as a tool for domination.

Resources: nan
Related Patterns:
Shared Stewardship, Consent, Not Consensus, Right Decision, Right Level
Right Decision, Right Level
(Unblock)
Right Decision, Right Level

Description: Aligns decision-making authority with the group best suited to act.

Problem: Decision-making at inappropriate levels creates bottlenecks or concentrates power inefficiently.

Solution: Align decision-making authority with the group or domain most equipped to handle it, ensuring responsiveness and contextual insight.

Resources: nan
Related Patterns:
Shared Stewardship, Consent, Not Consensus, Power as Resource, Not Authority
Naming the Invisible
(Unblock)
Naming the Invisible

Description: Makes implicit dynamics explicit to clarify roles and accountability.

Problem: Hidden assumptions and unspoken agreements lead to confusion and misaligned expectations.

Solution: Use visual tools and open dialogue to reveal and clarify invisible structures, roles, and accountability.

Resources: nan
Related Patterns:
Shared Stewardship, Consent, Not Consensus, Right Decision, Right Level
