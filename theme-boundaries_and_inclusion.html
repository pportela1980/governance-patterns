Boundaries and Inclusion

Impenetrable Boundaries
(Block)
Impenetrable Boundaries

Description: Rigid boundaries inhibit new participation and foster insularity.

Problem: Overly rigid boundaries can inhibit new participation and foster insularity, limiting growth and innovation.

Solution: Overprotecting group identity at the cost of openness leads to stagnation and groupthink.

Resources: nan
Related Patterns:
Permeable Boundaries, Invite, Don’t Oblige, Holding the Edge
Entry by Favoritism
(Block)
Entry by Favoritism

Description: Favoritism in entry processes undermines transparency and diversity.

Problem: Selecting new members based on personal relationships or favoritism undermines transparency and curtails diversity.

Solution: Exposes the risks of informal, exclusionary practices and calls for merit- and relationship-based entry processes.

Resources: nan
Related Patterns:
Entry by Relationship, Participation with Purpose
Closed Networks
(Block)
Closed Networks

Description: Exclusionary networks risk echo chambers and limit innovation.

Problem: Insular networks that exclude external voices risk becoming echo chambers, reinforcing biases and limiting innovation.

Solution: Highlights the dangers of maintaining overly exclusive networks that fail to adapt to external insights.

Resources: nan
Related Patterns:
Permeable Boundaries, Invite, Don’t Oblige, Holding the Edge
Permeable Boundaries
(Unblock)
Permeable Boundaries

Description: Creates flexible boundaries that welcome new ideas while preserving core values.

Problem: Overly rigid boundaries hinder growth by preventing fresh perspectives from entering the group.

Solution: Establish flexible boundaries that allow new ideas to enter while preserving essential values and focus.

Resources: nan
Related Patterns:
Impenetrable Boundaries, Closed Networks
Invite, Don’t Oblige
(Unblock)
Invite, Don’t Oblige

Description: Encourages voluntary, meaningful engagement instead of forced participation.

Problem: Forcing participation can lead to disengagement and resentment, reducing the quality of involvement.

Solution: Create engagement processes that invite voluntary, meaningful contributions rather than mandating participation.

Resources: nan
Related Patterns:
Permeable Boundaries, Holding the Edge
Holding the Edge
(Unblock)
Holding the Edge

Description: Develops spaces for external voices to interact with core processes.

Problem: Organizations often struggle to integrate external perspectives without compromising internal cohesion.

Solution: Develop “edge spaces” where outside voices can interact with core processes, bringing fresh insights while maintaining group integrity.

Resources: nan
Related Patterns:
Invite, Don’t Oblige, Permeable Boundaries
Trust is the Work
(Unblock)
Trust is the Work

Description: Emphasizes ongoing relational work as the foundation for effective governance.

Problem: Focusing solely on measurable outputs neglects the relational work that builds trust within an organization.

Solution: Make trust-building an explicit, ongoing practice by dedicating time and resources to relationship cultivation and transparent communication.

Resources: nan
Related Patterns:
Holding the Edge, Permeable Boundaries
