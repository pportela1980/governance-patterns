Transparency and Participation

The Black Box Decision
(Block)
The Black Box Decision

Description: Opaque decision-making erodes trust and excludes affected stakeholders.

Problem: When decisions are made behind closed doors, trust is eroded and those affected feel excluded.

Solution: A drive for efficiency at the expense of openness undermines accountability and inclusiveness.

Resources: nan
Related Patterns:
Making Power Visible, Participation with Purpose
Participation as Performance
(Block)
Participation as Performance

Description: Superficial participation creates an illusion of inclusion.

Problem: Superficial participatory processes give an appearance of inclusion while key decisions remain in the hands of a few.

Solution: Exposes how performative participation leads to disengagement and cynicism when genuine influence is lacking.

Resources: nan
Related Patterns:
Participation with Purpose, Entry by Relationship
The Token Inclusion Trap
(Block)
The Token Inclusion Trap

Description: Symbolic inclusion without real power-sharing undermines engagement.

Problem: Inviting stakeholders only symbolically—without transferring real decision-making authority—leads to frustration and undermines trust.

Solution: True inclusion requires transferring substantive decision-making authority rather than mere symbolism.

Resources: nan
Related Patterns:
Participation with Purpose, Learn in Public
Making Power Visible
(Unblock)
Making Power Visible

Description: Reveals hidden power dynamics to build trust and clarity.

Problem: Hidden power dynamics can allow inequity to fester and erode trust across the organization.

Solution: Actively reveal and clarify power structures via transparent role definitions, open decision-making, and visible accountability measures.

Resources: nan
Related Patterns:
The Black Box Decision, Participation with Purpose
Participation with Purpose
(Unblock)
Participation with Purpose

Description: Structures engagement to align with individuals’ strengths and impact.

Problem: Unstructured or superficial participation fails to tap into the full potential of a community’s skills and insights.

Solution: Design engagement processes that align participation with personal strengths and interests, ensuring meaningful and impactful contributions.

Resources: nan
Related Patterns:
Making Power Visible, Entry by Relationship, Learn in Public
Entry by Relationship
(Unblock)
Entry by Relationship

Description: Uses relational entry to foster organic, trust-based inclusion.

Problem: Formal, impersonal vetting processes can feel exclusionary and prevent valuable new perspectives from being integrated.

Solution: Prioritize relational entry methods that build trust through personal connections, welcoming new voices organically.

Resources: nan
Related Patterns:
Participation with Purpose, Making Power Visible
Learn in Public
(Unblock)
Learn in Public

Description: Encourages sharing successes and setbacks to foster collective learning.

Problem: When failures and challenges are hidden, organizations miss valuable opportunities for collective learning and growth.

Solution: Share both successes and setbacks openly to build a culture of continuous learning and improvement.

Resources: nan
Related Patterns:
Entry by Relationship, Participation with Purpose
